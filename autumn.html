Fukuoka, Japan

please tag along

Kyushu, with its extensive land, sea, and air transportation networks, is a region full of vibrant energy, cultural richness, and recreational opportunities. As Japan's third-largest island, it is famous for its tonkotsu ramen, soothing hot springs, varied mountain ranges, and tranquil beaches. Fukuoka has emerged as a global startup hub, while the volcanic landscapes in the south draw attention with their eruptions and smoke. The Beppu Onsen area, renowned for its hot springs, features eight steaming springs created by geological activity, all set against breathtaking natural scenery. This geological activity also provides impressive peaks for hiking, including Mount Kirishima. Kyushu seamlessly blends modern innovation with a laid-back lifestyle, making it an ideal destination for those seeking a slower pace. If you're looking to enjoy stunning nature, rich culture, and delicious cuisine, a leisurely trip to this southern island is just what you need.

Bangkok, Thailand

please tag along

The ideal time to visit Thailand is between November and March, when the weather is cool and dry. However, this period also coincides with peak tourist season, resulting in higher prices for accommodations and flights. The fall months, particularly September and October, tend to be quite rainy, especially in Bangkok. Despite the downpours, you can still have a great experience! Traveling during the off-season offers advantages such as fewer crowds and the chance to take advantage of low-season discounts, along with slightly cooler temperatures. As the monsoon season peaks in September and starts to ease by November, tourists begin to arrive to enjoy the sunnier days. Traditionally, November marks the beginning of the busy season in Thailand, although it doesn’t become particularly crowded until around Christmas.
