Oxford, England

please tag along

If you're planning to enjoy some of your spring in the UK, consider skipping the crowds in London and heading to Oxford instead. Capture a photo beside the renowned cherry blossom tree outside the University Church on High Street, explore the University’s botanical gardens or one of the city's many parks, or take a leisurely walk down the vibrant Cowley Road, where you can relax with a drink or two in the warm spring sun. For a truly memorable experience, visit on May 1st: on this day, bars and clubs remain open until 5 AM, and by 6 AM, thousands gather in the city center to listen to the church bells and choirs singing hymns from the Great Tower.

Venice, Italy

please tag along

I love Venice at any time of the year, but spring is my favorite season to visit. During this time, the weather begins to warm up after winter, remaining pleasantly cool without the humidity of July and August. You'll encounter far fewer crowds compared to the bustling summer months, and with longer days, there's more time to appreciate Venice's beauty. It's the perfect opportunity to stroll through charming streets, enjoy a gondola ride, and savor delicious local cuisine without the hassle of large tourist groups. Exploring nearby islands like Murano, Burano, and Lido is also easier, as the Vaporetto water buses tend to be less crowded, allowing you to relax and enjoy the scenic journey across the lagoon. Plus, accommodation prices are generally lower in spring, before the peak summer season drives up rates.
